## 1. Brand Essence

**One-line positioning:** The membership for people who don't want a membership. One pass, unlimited pickleball, every great club.

**Tagline:** *One pass. Play anywhere.*

**Mission:** Free pickleball players from single-club lock-in by creating a curated, cross-club network where one subscription works everywhere — and clubs win by filling courts they couldn't fill alone.

**Audience:**
- Primary: Active pickleball players, ages 25–45, urban/metro, DUPR 3.0–5.0, plays 2–6×/week, often at multiple venues
- Secondary: Travelers, relocators, weekend warriors, couples splitting clubs
- Partner-side: Independent club operators with off-peak inventory

## 2. Verbal Voice

### Tone signature
**Confident · Direct · Player-first · Quietly playful**

We sound like a player who built the thing they always wanted — not a SaaS company, not a fitness chain, not a tech bro. Energy without hype. Specific without jargon.

### Voice attributes (with sliders)

| Attribute        | We are…                  | Not…                           |
| ---------------- | ------------------------ | ------------------------------ |
| Energy           | Energetic, momentum-led  | Manic, exclamation-heavy       |
| Formality        | Casual, contraction-heavy| Corporate, stiff               |
| Authority        | Confident, evidence-led  | Boastful, hyperbolic           |
| Warmth           | Welcoming, inclusive     | Sappy, performative            |
| Humor            | Dry, situational         | Pun-heavy, meme-dependent      |
| Tech-forward     | Modern, clean            | Buzzwordy, AI-washed           |

### "We Are / We Are Not"

| ✅ We Are                                              | 🚫 We Are Not                                                |
| ----------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| Plain-spoken — "Skip the membership at every club."   | Marketingese — "Unlock a paradigm-shifting pickleball ecosystem." |
| Specific — "120+ partner clubs in 14 metros."         | Vague — "Many top-tier clubs in select markets."           |
| Player-side first — court access is the hero          | Operator-side jargon — "yield-managed inventory liquidity" |
| Confident in the wedge — "Stop paying to belong"      | Apologetic or hedging — "We hope to maybe offer..."         |
| Pro-club — clubs are partners, not commodities        | Anti-club — DinkPass is not a "disruptor" of clubs         |
| Honest about limits — fair-use caps, indoor/outdoor   | Falsely "unlimited" — never bait-and-switch                |
| Inclusive of skill levels                             | Tournament-bro coded                                        |
| Numbers-driven (24M, $0, 120+, ∞)                     | Vibes-only                                                  |

### Signature phrases & vocabulary

**Use freely:**
- *One pass. Play anywhere.* (master tagline)
- *Show up, scan, play.* (the experience reduced to 3 verbs)
- *Couch to court.* (acquisition story)
- *Skip the membership.*
- *Stop paying to belong. Start paying to play.* (philosophy)
- *Founding member*, *partner club*, *home metro*, *travel mode*
- *Open play*, *court time*, *session*, *match*
- *Curated, not crowded.*

**Use sparingly:**
- *Unlimited* — always pair with the fair-use caveat in any contract-adjacent context
- *Free* — only for genuinely free things (waitlist, guest passes, paddle rentals on National)

**Don't use:**
- "Game-changer," "revolutionize," "disrupt," "frictionless," "seamless"
- "Hey pickleballers!" — never address the audience as a category
- "World-class," "premium," "elite" (let specifics carry the weight)
- "Pickleballer" as a noun — say "player" or "members"
- Heavy emoji or pickleball-pun overload (one pun per page max)

### Sentence construction
- **Short sentences win.** "Walk in, scan your QR pass, hit the court." beats a 30-word version every time.
- **Active voice.** "Book in seconds" not "Bookings can be made quickly."
- **Numerals over words** for stats and prices ($89, 24M, 120+, 14 metros).
- **One idea per sentence** in headlines; nuance lives in the subhead.
- **Use em-dashes and "—" generously** for asides; avoid semicolons.

## 3. Messaging Architecture

### Three messaging pillars

1. **Freedom from lock-in**
   *Why:* Players resent paying full memberships at multiple clubs.
   *Proof:* "Skip the membership at every club." · "$0 sign-up fees." · "One pass."

2. **Curated network, not a directory**
   *Why:* Quality over quantity is the trust play vs. ClassPass-style breadth.
   *Proof:* "120+ partner clubs, vetted for quality, vibe, and fair court access." · "Curated, not crowded."

3. **Fair to clubs, fair to players**
   *Why:* The two-sided proposition has to feel honest from both directions.
   *Proof:* Visit caps stated openly · Per-visit payouts · Members keep their home club, just gain everywhere else.

### Tone-by-context matrix

| Context                       | Tone                                  | Example opener                                              |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| Landing hero                  | Bold, declarative                     | "One pass. Play anywhere."                                  |
| Pricing                       | Plain, unembellished, scannable       | "Simple plans. Unlimited play."                             |
| Onboarding email              | Warm, brief, action-led               | "You're in. Here's how to book your first session."         |
| Push notification             | Crisp, contextual                     | "Court 4 just opened at Bounce — 7:00 PM."                  |
| Club operator pitch           | Numbers-first, partner-respecting     | "Fill empty courts. Keep your members."                     |
| Support / failure modes       | Calm, owning, specific next step      | "We couldn't hold that court — here's the next slot."       |
| Social (organic)              | Player POV, dry humor                 | "Three club memberships. Now I just have one."              |
| Press / investor              | Numbers + thesis, no fluff            | "24M players. 120 clubs. One pass."                          |
| Product copy (buttons)        | Verb-first, 1–3 words                 | "Reserve free" · "Get early access" · "Claim my spot"       |

## 4. Visual Identity

### Color palette

| Role             | Name        | Hex       | Usage                                            |
| ---------------- | ----------- | --------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| Primary text     | **Ink**     | `#0B1411` | Default text, dark sections, primary CTA bg      |
| Background       | **Cream**   | `#F6F1E7` | Page background — never pure white               |
| Accent (loud)    | **Lime 400**| `#9DE054` | Pill highlights, ring accents, "founding" badge  |
| Accent (action)  | **Lime 500**| `#7FD13A` | Buttons on dark, pulsing dots, success states    |
| Accent (deep)    | **Lime 700**| `#3F7A18` | Eyebrow / overline text on cream                 |
| Brand neutral    | **Court**   | `#1F4E3D` | Hero court visual, partner-section bg            |
| Soft fill        | **Lime 100**| `#E1F6C5` | Pill backgrounds, subtle highlights              |

**Rules:**
- Cream is the default page background — pure white only inside cards.
- Lime is for accent only; never set body type in lime.
- Ink-on-cream and lime-on-ink are the two highest-contrast pairings — use one of them in every hero.
- Court green is reserved for hero/partner moments — don't sprinkle it.

### Typography

| Role     | Family    | Weights         | Notes                                                            |
| -------- | --------- | --------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Display  | **Fraunces** | 600, 800     | Headlines, stats, pricing. Letter-spacing **−0.02em**. Italic axis available — use sparingly. |
| Body     | **Inter**    | 400, 500, 600, 700 | All UI, paragraphs, buttons, nav.                          |

**Rules:**
- Headlines lean huge — `text-5xl` to `text-7xl` with `leading-[0.95]`.
- Body sits at `text-base` / `text-lg` with `leading-relaxed`.
- Eyebrows are uppercase Inter, `tracking-[0.25em]`, `text-xs`, `font-semibold`, in lime-700 (light bg) or lime-300 (dark bg).
- Numbers use Fraunces — they're a brand asset.

### UI primitives

| Element           | Spec                                                                 |
| ----------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Card              | `rounded-3xl` (24px), `bg-white` or `bg-ink/5`, `ring-1 ring-ink/10`, `shadow-soft` |
| Soft shadow       | `0 1px 0 rgba(0,0,0,0.04), 0 8px 30px -12px rgba(0,0,0,0.18)`        |
| Buttons (primary) | `rounded-full`, `bg-ink text-cream`, `px-6 py-3.5`, `font-semibold`  |
| Buttons (accent)  | `rounded-full`, `bg-lime-400 text-ink`                               |
| Inputs            | `rounded-full`, `bg-white`, `ring-1 ring-ink/10`, focus `ring-lime-500` |
| Badges/pills      | `rounded-full`, `bg-lime-100 text-lime-700 ring-1 ring-lime-300/60`  |
| Dividers          | `border-ink/10` (cream sections) · `border-white/10` (ink sections)  |

**Always rounded-full for interactive elements.** No square buttons. No sharp corners on cards.

### Decorative motifs

- **Underline highlight** behind key words: `bg-lime-300/80` rectangle under the word, `-z-0` so text rides on top.
- **Floating cards** with slight rotation (`rotate-[-4deg]`, `rotate-[3deg]`) overlapping hero visuals — feels editorial, not stock.
- **Court pattern**: linear-gradient grid overlaying court-green, masked with a radial fade — used as a sport-coded backdrop.
- **Grain texture**: subtle dot pattern on cream backgrounds for warmth — never on dark.
- **Pulsing map pins**: lime dot + ring + `animate-pulse` for "live" energy.
- **Animated marquee** of partner names — `mask-image` linear-gradient fade on edges; never abrupt cut-offs.

### Imagery direction

- **People over equipment.** Show players mid-rally, mid-laugh, mid-handshake. Avoid solo "hero paddle" shots.
- **Real clubs, real light.** No stock studios. Honor the indoor-warehouse, outdoor-court, rooftop-bar variety.
- **Diversity is non-negotiable.** Age, race, body type, skill level. Pickleball is multi-generational and we show it.
- **Composition leans wide and cinematic** — courts are horizontal spaces; let them breathe.
- **No trophies, no fist-pumps.** This isn't a "winners" brand. It's a "show up and play" brand.

### Iconography
- 1.5–2.2px stroke, rounded line caps, geometric.
- Lucide-style is the default. Pickleball-specific: a paddle silhouette + dimpled ball.
- Never use color icons — monochrome on accent backgrounds only.

## 5. Application Examples

### Headline patterns
- `[One thing.] [Another thing.]` → "One pass. Play anywhere." / "Simple plans. Unlimited play."
- `[Stop X.] [Start Y.]` → "Stop paying to belong. Start paying to play."
- `[Three steps from X to Y.]` → "Three steps from couch to court."

### CTA patterns
| Stage           | Copy                              |
| --------------- | --------------------------------- |
| Top of funnel   | "Get early access →"              |
| Pricing         | "Start Local Pass" · "Go National"|
| Final           | "Claim my spot"                   |
| In-product      | "Reserve free" · "Book this slot" |
| Operator        | "Partner with DinkPass →"         |

### Email subject line examples
- ✅ "You're in. 50% off, locked in for life."
- ✅ "Bounce just joined DinkPass."
- ✅ "Court 4 opened up — 7 PM, two miles away."
- 🚫 "🥒 BIG NEWS!! Don't miss this exclusive pickleball offer 🚀🚀"

### Push notifications
- ✅ "3 spots left at DETA, 6:30 PM."
- ✅ "Maya invited you to a 3.5+ session tonight."
- 🚫 "Hey pickleballer! Don't forget to play!"

### Don't write this
- ❌ "Welcome to the future of pickleball."
- ❌ "Our state-of-the-art platform leverages AI…"
- ❌ "Get unlimited pickleball today!!! 🥒🎾🔥"
- ❌ "DinkPass is revolutionizing the way America plays."

### Do write this
- ✅ "One pass. Every great club in your city. Cancel anytime."
- ✅ "We pay clubs per visit so they actually want you there."
- ✅ "120+ clubs at launch. More every week."

## 6. Brand Boundaries

**Always:**
- Disclose fair-use caps when "unlimited" appears in marketing
- Credit clubs by name when featured (logo + locale)
- Lead with the player benefit, then the network proof
- Use real numbers ($89, 14 metros, 120+ clubs) — update when they change

**Never:**
- Frame DinkPass as anti-club or club-replacing
- Compare directly to The Picklr / Life Time / specific chains by name in marketing
- Use a player's name, photo, or DUPR rating without written consent
- Claim partnerships before LOIs are signed
- Use "premium," "luxury," or "elite" — we're for everyone who plays

## 8. Open Questions

1. **Brand name:** Is "DinkPass" the working name or the final name? If it changes, search/replace across this doc + landing page.
2. **Audience priority:** Is the primary buyer the multi-club regular, the traveler, or the new-to-the-sport player? This affects pricing emphasis and acquisition messaging.
3. **Voice on operator-side surfaces:** Should the club-facing tone match the player tone, or feel more "B2B SaaS" (e.g., contracts, dashboards)? Recommendation: same tone, fewer puns, more numbers.
4. **Geographic emphasis at launch:** All 14 metros equally, or one anchor metro for messaging concentration?
5. **Pickleball-specific vocabulary:** How heavily do we lean into in-group terms ("dink," "kitchen," "third shot drop")? Recommend: light flavor, never a barrier to non-players.
6. **Imagery — DUPR partnership:** If we integrate DUPR for skill ratings, do we co-brand visually, or keep DUPR as a back-end utility?
7. **Founding-member offer durability:** ✅ Resolved → switched from "50% off for life" to "30% off for the first 12 months" (protects long-term margin; still strong early-adopter signal).
